# Product Foundations

The first principles of building the right thing. Lean by design — the single product hub
([[lead-product-manager]]) applies these; the depth (frameworks, GTM, metrics instrumentation) lives in
its spokes.

## Outcomes over output
Ship *changes in user/business behavior*, not features. A roadmap of outputs ("build X") with no target
outcome is a wish list. Every initiative names the outcome and the leading indicator that proves it.

## Jobs to be done
Users "hire" a product to make progress in a situation. Design for the **job and its context**, not the
demographic or the feature request. The competitor is whatever they'd hire instead — including doing nothing.

## Evidence over opinion
Confidence is a tier, not a feeling — name it (evidenced / industry-supported / expert judgment /
preference) and calibrate advocacy to it. Discovery exists to *cheaply invalidate* ideas before
expensive building. The strongest PM move is killing a plausible idea with evidence.

## The four risks
Every meaningful bet is tested against **value** (will they want it?), **usability** (can they use it?),
**feasibility** (can we build it?), and **viability** (should the business do it?). Most failures are a
value or viability miss dressed up as an execution problem.

## Prioritization under constraint
Strategy is choosing what *not* to do. Sequence by leverage (outcome impact ÷ cost/confidence), protect
focus, and make the trade-offs explicit. A prioritization with no "no" is just a backlog.
